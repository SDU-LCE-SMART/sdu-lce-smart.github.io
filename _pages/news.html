AGRO4AGRI Project, Consortium Meeting M24

28–29 April 2026, Milan, Italy

On 28–29 April 2026, the AGRO4AGRI consortium held its M24 meeting in Milan, Italy. The event was kindly hosted by Sipcam Oxon at their formulation site, providing an excellent setting for collaboration, technical discussions, and knowledge exchange among project partners.

During the meeting, SDU, together with colleagues from the Technical University of Denmark (DTU) and the Institute of Advanced Studies (IHS), presented recent progress within Work Package 7: Environmental Safety and Socioeconomic Assessment. Updates were delivered across the main tasks, including safety and toxicity assessment, with a focus on Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD), early environmental sustainability assessment, and social and economic impact analysis.

SDU presented preliminary Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) results for various slow-release fertilizer systems based on both organic and inorganic carriers. These results were evaluated within the SSbD performance scoring framework, allowing the identification of key environmental hotspots and areas for potential improvement.

As next steps, SDU will update the LCA models based on newly available laboratory data and continue collecting data related to dsRNA production and encapsulation processes.

Overall, the meeting highlighted the steady progress of the project and the increasing maturity of the developed technologies. AGRO4AGRI continues to advance within a dynamic context shaped by scientific innovation as well as evolving market and policy conditions.

ALLIANCE First General Assembly Meeting

9 April 2026

The first General Assembly Meeting of the ALLIANCE project was successfully held online on 9 April 2026, bringing together project partners to align on objectives, share progress, and plan upcoming activities.

During the meeting, each work package presented its ongoing activities. SDU, as WP1 leader for safety, toxicity, environmental, economic, and social assessment, highlighted key advancements achieved during the first eight months of the project. These activities were primarily driven by SDU-led tasks at this stage of the project timeline, including:

  • A Data Compilation Strategy (DCS), aligned with FAIR principles, has been developed to ensure consistent data collection and secure exchange across WPs 2–4.
  • Scoping and system model description have been defined to implement the first stage of the Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) framework as a redesign process, ensuring that safety and sustainability evaluations focus on the appropriate parameters from the beginning of the project.

In addition, an interactive advisory board workshop was conducted, where partners collaboratively identified key topics, expertise needs, and questions for future engagement with external experts.

The meeting provided an important platform for strengthening collaboration and ensuring alignment across technical, environmental, and socio-economic aspects of the project. It marked a strong start for ALLIANCE and established a clear direction for the upcoming phases.

MARMADE Steering Board and Project Meeting

1 April 2026

The MARMADE Steering Board and Project Meeting was successfully held online on 1 April 2026, bringing together project partners to review progress, exchange updates, and coordinate upcoming activities.

During the meeting, each work package presented its ongoing activities and next steps. SDU, as leader of WP5, presented the objectives and status of the sustainability assessment activities, focusing on comprehensive environmental Life Cycle Assessment (LCA), social Life Cycle Assessment (s-LCA), and techno-economic assessment, including TEA and Life Cycle Costing (LCC), within the Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) framework.

SDU also highlighted the main WP5 tasks, and the defined LCA scenarios were presented as an important step towards developing the sustainability assessment framework and aligning it with the technical work packages.

The meeting provided an important platform for strengthening collaboration and ensuring alignment across technical, environmental, social, and economic aspects of the project. It supported clear coordination among partners and helped define the direction for the next phases of MARMADE.

Sustainability Assessment & Life Cycle Thinking Workshop

17–18 November 2025

Our team hosted a two-day Sustainability Assessment & Life Cycle Thinking Workshop at the Department of Green Technology, University of Southern Denmark, organized within the framework of the AgriLoop project. The workshop aimed to strengthen knowledge and practical skills in sustainability assessment methods used to evaluate emerging technologies and bio-based systems.

Day 1 – Lectures and Knowledge Building
The first day focused on key theoretical concepts in sustainability assessment. The lectures covered topics including Systems Thinking, Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD), Life Cycle Assessment (LCA), Material Flow Analysis (MFA), agricultural emissions and land-use change, and Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM). The sessions encouraged active discussion and exchange of ideas among participants.

Day 2 – Hands-on Learning
The second day focused on practical training. Participants worked in groups on Life Cycle Assessment case studies, practicing consequential LCA, using the Activity Browser, interpreting results, and presenting their findings at the end of the day.

The workshop provided an interactive platform for learning, collaboration, and strengthening capacity in sustainability assessment and life cycle thinking.

European Energy Innovation Magazine

The TAKE-OFF project was featured in the last edition of the European Energy Innovation magazine in an article titled: TAKE-OFF and the techno-economic and environmental assessment of jet fuel production from CO₂ and H₂.

Our project’s pathways for producing sustainable aviation fuel (#SAF) from CO₂ and H₂ are currently at a low technology readiness level (TRL 3-4) and are expected to reach TRL 5 by the end of the project. To include sustainability aspects in the early stages of design and production, all technology lines are assessed in terms of sustainability using Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) and Techno-economic Analysis (TEA).

Multiple scenarios regarding carbon capture, hydrogen production technology, energy sources, etc. are developed and system models for each scenario are created and simulated using experimental data.

The article was drafted by Benyamin Khoshnevisan and Hadis Marami from our project partner Syddansk Universitet - University of Southern Denmark.
