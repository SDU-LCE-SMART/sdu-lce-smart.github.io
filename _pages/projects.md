## AgriLoop 

**Pushing the frontier of circular agriculture by converting residues into novel economic, social and environmental opportunities**

<img src="/images/agriloop.png" class="project-image">

**Duration:** 01/2023 – 12/2026  
**Funding:** EU Horizon Europe  

**About the Project**  
AgriLoop is an international research project that aims to increase the value of agricultural residues by converting them into high-value bio-based products. Through a cascading biorefinery approach, the project develops safe-and-sustainable-by-design (SSbD) processes to transform residues from sectors such as tomato, soy, straw, potato, brewery, and livestock into products including plant and microbial proteins, biopolymers, and other bio-based chemicals for food, feed, health, and material applications. By strengthening collaboration between European Union and Chinese partners, AgriLoop seeks to improve resource efficiency, reduce waste from agriculture, and support the development of sustainable bio-based markets.

**Keywords:** Biorefinery, Bioeconomy, Agri-residues 

**Project Link:**  
[https://www.agriloop-project.eu/](https://www.agriloop-project.eu/)

## FLAVOURFERM 
**Unleashing the flavour potential of plant-based foods via fermentation**

<img src="/images/flavourferm.png" class="project-image">

**Duration:** 11/2024 – 04/2028  
**Funding:** EU Horizon Europe 

**About the Project**  
FLAVOURFERM aims to optimize, demonstrate, and deploy fermentation technologies to unlock the flavor potential of plant-based foods and respond to consumer demand for tasty, nutritious, and healthy plant-based products. Precision fermentation, biomass fermentation, and traditional fermentation techniques will be optimized to improve the sensory properties (flavor and texture) as well as the protein content and bioavailability in plant-based foods. Innovative fermented foods will be developed by incorporating ingredients from the optimized fermentation techniques into food products. The production will involve animal-free recombinant casein obtained by precision fermentation, as well as fungal mycelia obtained from legume fermentation, to produce an array of novel food products in three business cases: plant-based cheese, plant-based meat, and plant-based milk. All novel ingredients and final products will be scaled-up and produced at pilot and pre-commercial scale in collaboration with industrial partners, which include both innovative SMEs and established manufacturers from different EU regions.

**Keywords:** Plant-based foods, Alternative proteins, Fermentation

**Project Link:**  
[https://www.flavourferm.eu/](https://www.flavourferm.eu/)

## AGRO4AGRI 
**FOSTERING THE ADVANCED USE OF AGROCHEMICALS FOR A SUSTAINABLE AGRICULTURE**

<img src="/images/agro4agri.png" class="project-image">

**Duration:** 05/2024 – 04/2028  
**Funding:** the European Union

**About the Project**  
AGRO4AGRI is an EU-funded project set to revolutionize plant nutrition and protection through cutting-edge nano and biotechnology. Within 4 years, by enhancing fertilizer efficiency and developing species-specific nematicides, the project aims to deliver sustainable and innovative solutions under the Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) framework. The project focuses on implementing nano and biobased controlled delivery systems for fertilizers, combined with plant biostimulants, to boost nutrient use efficiency. Additionally, it aims to develop target-specific biopesticides using RNAi technology, which are safe for humans and non-target organisms. AGRO4AGRI’s solutions are designed to be biodegradable and environmentally friendly, reducing the need for traditional agrochemicals. Aligned with the European Green Deal, the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP), the Farm to Fork Strategy, and the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals, AGRO4AGRI aims to cut nutrient and pesticide use in agriculture by over 40% and 50%, respectively.

**Keywords:** AI, LCA, Decision Support  

**Project Link:**  
[https://agro4agri.eu/](https://agro4agri.eu/)

## MARMADE 
**MARine bioMass valorizAtion for fooD and fEed innovation**

<img src="/images/marmade.png" class="project-image">

**Duration:** 09/2025 – 09/2029  
**Funding:** European Union – Horizon Europe, under the Circular Bio-based Europe Joint Undertaking (CBE JU), as a HORIZON Research and Innovation Action (HORIZON-RIA)

**About the Project**  
MARMADE is creating a sustainable and innovative bio-based value chain for turning marine biomass into high-value food and feed ingredients. The aim is to valorize crustacean residues (shrimp and blue crab shells) and seaweed to develop affordable, nutritious, safe, and sustainable ingredients. Through advanced biorefinery processes, the project converts these underutilized marine resources into a portfolio of high-value bio-based products in a cascading extraction approach. These valuable extracts include pre/postbiotics, vitamins, peptides, oligosaccharides, fats, emulsifiers, and digestibility enhancers. The final products are innovative food and feed prototypes with improved nutritional and sensory properties. The entire process is rigorously checked for compliance with safety, sustainability, and regulatory standards, integrating the Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) framework.

**Keywords:** Food and feed fish ingredients, Biorefinery approach, Cascading extraction processes

**Project Link:**  
[https://marmade-project.eu/](https://marmade-project.eu/)

## ZDHydro
**Development of an Integrated Zero Discharge Model for Sewage Sludge to Enhanced Hydrogen Production**

**Duration:** 01/2023 – 04/2027  
**Funding:** Innovation Fund Danmark

**About the Project**  
This project focuses on converting sewage sludge, which is an abundant and problematic waste stream in both India and Denmark, into a valuable source of hydrogen (H₂) within an effort to push transition towards a circular economy framework. Sewage sludge is valorized through a combination of emerging biohydrogen production technologies, including dark fermentation (DF), microbial electrolysis cells (MEC), and photofermentation (PF), while addressing key limitations that currently hinder large-scale application, such as low substrate conversion, process instability, and by-product formation. ZDHydro investigates microbial community dynamics, optimizes substrate selection and pretreatment strategies as well as test potential synergistic effects of co-fermentation with organic fraction of municipal solid waste. By combining DF, MEC, and PF into an advanced DMPI system, the project aims to significantly improve hydrogen yields and process efficiency. The resulting integrated approach is to be validated at pilot scale (100 L) and amply assessed using a techno-economic and environmental assessment framework to ensure viability and demonstrate environmental benefits over tradeoffs.


**Keywords:** biohydrogen production, sewage sludge valorization, waste-to-energy

**Project Link:**  
[https://agro4agri.eu/](https://agro4agri.eu/)

## ALLIANCE
**(EUROPEAN MICROALGAE ALLIANCE): INNOVATIVE, SAFE, AND SUSTAINABLE MULTIPRODUCT BIOREFINERIES FOR A BLUE BIOBASED ECONOMY.**

<img src="/images/alliance.png" class="project-image">

**Duration:** 09/2025 – 08/2029  
**Funding:** ALLIANCE is supported by the Circular Bio-based Europe Joint Undertaking and its member, and co-funded by the EU (Grant No. 101214199).  

**About the Project**  
The ALLIANCE project aims to accelerate the uptake of microalgae-based products in the European market by enhancing cost-effectiveness, circularity, and sustainability across production and processing. Guided by the Safe and Sustainable by Design (SSbD) framework, it focuses on developing mid-value algae-based ingredients through multi-product biorefineries serving food, cellular agriculture, aquafeed, and agriculture sectors. Key innovations include nutrient and water recirculation, automated and efficient production systems, reduced resource consumption, renewable and off-grid energy use, and solvent-free biorefinery processes. ALLIANCE integrates upstream and downstream processes across four production pipelines, expanding them into 15 near-zero-waste product lines. Advanced wet biomass processing and solvent-free extraction improve efficiency and scalability. Supported by a multidisciplinary consortium of 19 European partners, the project combines technological innovation with market and policy engagement to enable sustainable commercialization and strengthen Europe’s bio-based economy.


**Keywords:** ALLIANCE, Microalgae, MPB (multi-product biorefineries)

**Project Link:**  
[https://microalgae-alliance.eu/ ](https://microalgae-alliance.eu/ )
